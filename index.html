About Us

It was the school days, while fellow students were focused on choosing between arts and engineering, we were quite an exception. 'Youngsters and social responsibilities never go together'. We wanted to prove the myth wrong. That’s when we collected money from our parents and visited a nearby orphanage. It was that life changing visit which ignited our minds and hearts towards serving the society. We then went on to help the people affected by Uttarakhand floods(2013). Helping became a habit and we continued our service for over 7 years. We wanted to extend our helping hands to more people and realised the need for Jatayu Charitable Trust. Hence Jatayu Charitable Trust was formed in May 2020. Instead of seeking for solutions outside we decided to be the solution. Hence we took the motto ‘Be the change you seek’. We serve not just to help others but also to discover ourself in that process.

More than the money we give or the work we do, it is the humanity in us that matters.

Our Vision

To give back to the society that benefited us with opportunities and to encourage / motivate fellow youngsters to be a part of our movement which successfully solves social problems and breaks the myth that youngsters never go with social responsibilities.

Jatayu not only aims to Serve but to make them Serve

Our Projects

Jatayu, beyond being an NGO, was the idea of a group of youngsters who wanted to bring in a change in the society. Though Jatayu, now officially called as JATAYU CHARITABLE TRUST(JCT) was registered in 2020, the founder members of JCT as a group have been serving the society since 2013.

The group of youngsters who got together to help the people of Uttarkhand during the Uttarkhand Floods,2013, then went on to work for projects like
  • Vivekananda Rally
  • Helpie Selfie Challenge (an initiative to help the people of Kashmir during the 2014 floods)
  • Vidhaikalam (an extensive initiative to plant saplings), Thalir (2015)
  • Disaster relief measures during Chennai Floods(2015), Kerala Floods(2018) and Gaja Cyclone(2018).
  • Educational workshops in a tribal village in Hosur(2019)
  • Joint hands with LMES to provide their kits to an orphanage.

Our Journey So Far

2013

Ambattur TO Uttarakhand

This was the first project of JATAYU. We wanted to help the people suffering due to the devastating floods at Uttarakhand. So we collected clothes from people in and around Ambattur. We were able to collect about one and half tons of clothes.

2013

Vivekananda Rally

This was the second big project for us. Jatayu joined hands with the Ramakrishna Mission to unite 1800 youngsters marking the 150th anniversary of Swami Vivekananda. The youngsters were all dressed up like Vivekananda himself.

2014

Blood Camp

This was our third project. We joined hands with MIOT hospitals to conduct a blood camp in sethu bhaskara school, ambattur.We were able to bring 45 donors.

2014

Helpie Selfiee Challenge

We worked on this project along with The Raindropss, in order to aid the Prime Minister's relief fund assisting the Kashmir floods in September 2014.

2015

Clause For a Cause

We joined hands with CHILD an orphanage in madhananguppam and celebrated Christmas together with the kids.We satisfied a wish for each of the children present there.

2015

Vidhai Kalam

We distributed 8400 saplings in and around ambattur to promote greenery and to do our bit in realizing Dr.APJ Abdul kalam’s dream also to impart the culture of growing plants.

2015

Chennai Flood Relief

We volunteered to collect materials from different sources and distribute it directly to the flood affected areas during the Chennai Flood 2015.

2015

Thalir

We visited nearby Government school where the students were made to plant saplings within school premises and took the responsibility for its maintenance.

2016

Pongal Celebrations

We joined hands with CHILD an orphanage in madhananguppam and celebrated Pongal together with the inmates.

2017

Education Workshop

We joined hands with LMES and provided their kits to CHILD orphanage.

2018

Kerala Flood Relief

We volunteered to collect materials from different sources and distribute it directly to the flood affected areas during the Kerala Flood 2018.

2018

GAJA Cyclone Flood Relief

We volunteered to collect materials from different sources and distribute it directly to the affected areas during the GAJA Cyclone 2018.

2019

Educational Workshop in a Tribal village in Hosur

Planned and conducted a workshop in a Tribal Village in Hosur.
